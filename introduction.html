What is Poverty?

Poverty is about not having enough money to meet basic needs, including food, clothing, and shelter. However, poverty is more than just not having enough money. It is a state or condition in which a person or community lacks the financial resources and essentials for aminimum standard of living. Poverty means that the income level from employment is so low that basic human needs cannot be met.

Poverty-stricken people and families might go without proper housing, clean water, healthy food, and medical attention. When people are excluded within a society, when they are not well educated and have a higher incidence of illness, there are negative consequences for society. We all pay the price for poverty. The increased cost on the health system, the justice system, and other systems that support those living in poverty impact our economy.

Causes of Poverty

• Low agricultural sector

• High volume of inequality

• Increased population rates

• High levels of population growth

• Weakness in employment generation

• High inflation during crisis periods

• Weakness in the quality of jobs generated

• Low growth elasticity of poverty reduction

• Failure to fully develop the agriculture sector

• Low to moderate economic growth for the past 40 years

• High and persistent levels of inequality (incomes and assets), which dampen the positive impacts of economic expansion and recurrent shocks and exposure to risks such as economic crisis, conflicts, natural disasters, and "environmental poverty."

Effects of Poverty

• Inability to Afford Housing

With poverty plaguing the country and scarce employment opportunities, many Filipinos cannot afford housing, which puts them in danger of turning to the streets for accommodation.

• Malnutrition in the Philippines

Hunger is one of the extreme effects of poverty in the Philippines. With little money to buy food, Filipinos have to survive on minimal food; even when food supplies are stable, they are most accessible in other areas where people have enough income to purchase the food.

• Child Labor

With poverty taking a toll on Filipinos, parents often cannot make enough money to support their families; children then have to be taken out of school to work in harsh conditions.

• Crime and Thievery

People often resort to crime and thievery with conditions so troublesome to survive. There are too many people and not enough resources. Moreover, people become desperate and practice drastic measures to provide for themselves and their families with such conditions.
